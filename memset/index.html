How it works

Three ideas keep the system sustainable.

Memset doesn't replace your notebook, flashcards, or course. It sits on top and answers one question well: what should I revisit today?

1. Memsets

A Memset is a lightweight pointer to something you're learning — a page, a chapter, a deck, or a lecture.

2. Strategies

A Strategy defines the review rhythm — how intervals grow or repeat over time.

3. Categories

Categories cap how many related reviews can land on the same day, so nothing ever piles up.

Questions

Short answers, no fluff.

If something isn't here, the support email below goes straight to a real inbox.

Is Memset a flashcard app?
No. Memset is a review planner. You keep learning from your notebooks, books, decks or courses — Memset only tracks what to revisit and when.
What happens if I miss a day?
Items go overdue, your streak may interrupt, and you can recover without the whole schedule collapsing. One bad day shouldn't mean a week of backlog.
How are repetitions scheduled?
Each Memset has a Strategy that defines the interval rhythm. Categories then cap how many items of one kind can land on the same day.
Is there a free plan?
Yes — up to 3 Memsets, 3 Categories and 1 custom Strategy. The paid plan unlocks unlimited creation across all three, with monthly and annual options (plus an annual trial).
Which languages are supported?
English, Spanish and Ukrainian at launch. More locales will follow.
